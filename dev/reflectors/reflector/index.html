Reflectors

Ideal Point Reflectors

We define an ideal point reflectors with reflection coefficients, $\mathsf{\alpha}_1,\mathsf{\alpha}_2,…\mathsf{\alpha}_n$ located at fixed positions, say $\bm{ξ₁},\bm{ξ₂},…\bm{ξₙ}$ as follows

\[\mathsf{f}(\bm{\xi}) = \sum_{n} \mathsf{\alpha}_n \delta(\bm{\xi} - \bm{\xi}_n).\]

Note that the reflection coefficients take negative values since we assume that the voltage is fixed at the reflector's end. Mathematically, we define the reflection due to the LTI Omnidirectional Source as follows

\[\mathsf{r}(\bm{\xi},t) = \mathsf{f}(\bm{\xi}) \mathsf{q}(\bm{\xi},t).\]

Continuous Reflectors as a Line Segment

We define a simple continuous reflector in term of a line segment, $AB$ of length, $L$ as follows

\[\mathsf{f}(\bm{\xi}) = \int_{0}^{L}\mathsf{\alpha}_0 \delta(\bm{\xi} - [\bm{\xi}_0+k\bm{u}]) \mathrm{d}k\]

where $\mathsf{\alpha}_0$ is a reflection coefficient, $\bm{ξ₀}$ is an initial position vector, $\bm{u}$ is an unit vector in the direction of $AB$ and $k$ is any scalar quantity.
